Tell us about your news

Answer the questions below — the more detail you give, the stronger the story we can build. There are no right or wrong answers at this stage.

The event
Describe the time-bound event being announced. What person, company, or product did what thing, and when?
Who does this impact — and can you put a number on it? People affected, money involved, time saved, scale of change.
What was the situation before this happened? What changes now, and what comes next? Give us the arc.
Companies, brands, individuals — anyone with a media profile, social following, or market authority. Even if they're peripheral to the story.
Context & angle
What's already on record that this news continues or extends? Is this the first in a series, a follow-up to a previous announcement, or connected to something your company has said publicly before?
e.g. "Third partnership this year, following Barclays and Lloyds" / "We predicted this in our 2024 SME Finance Report" / "First partnership — nothing on record before this."
A superlative or unusual twist that makes this stand out from similar announcements. Think: firsts, lasts, biggests, smallests, fastests, furthests, or a surprising contrast.
e.g. "First fintech integrated with HSBC's SME platform" / "Fastest invoice turnaround in the UK at 4 hours" / "12-person startup partnering with the UK's largest business bank."
Industry themes, news hooks, recent media coverage, or competitor activity that gives this story wider context.
Assets
Spokespeople from your company and any third parties. Include job titles. Who has final approval on the release?
Portrait-style images of the business leader are essential for editorial coverage. Corporate headshots don't work — we need natural, characterful portraits.
Third-party stats or market data that contextualise the significance of the news. Include a source link where possible.

We'll analyse your answers and return an honest impact assessment — what's strong, what could be stronger, and our recommended approach.
